About

With over 20 years leadership and management experience in higher education and local government libraries, Liz Burke brings a breadth of knowledge and expertise to her consultancy work. Liz is passionate about the central role of libraries to their community, believing that a dynamic library is at the heart of every great community. Liz has an open and consultative communication style and is focused on supporting libraries to thrive.


In the five years leading up to Liz’s departure as Manager, Library & Museum Services at Grey District Council - the number of active borrowers more than doubled, the number of electronic items loaned more than tripled, and the number of virtual library visits increased by thirty-nine times. An effectively led library can contribute to the social, economic, environmental and cultural wellbeing of the community.


Prior to this Liz was Director, Library & Information Services at Murdoch University. Managing the merged University Library and IT Department comprising over 200 staff across three campus sites, and an international presence in Singapore. Liz’s many roles have included Associate University Librarian at The University of Western Australia, responsible for the delivery of library services across nine subject libraries.


Actively engaged in multiple leadership roles and professional activities Liz has served on the Kōtui Managers Executive Group, one year as Convenor; as a member of the Council of Australian University Librarians and the Council of Australian Directors of IT; on the Advisory Board of the Department of Information Studies at Curtin University; and a member of the Programme Committee for the EDUCAUSE Conference.


Liz earned a Bachelor of Social Sciences (Honours) in Library & Information Science at RMIT; a Master of Information Management & Systems from Monash University; and a Bachelor of Letters with a major in Art History from The University of Melbourne.


Liz has Professional Registration from the Library & Information Association of New Zealand Aotearoa, and is an alumni of the Aurora Foundation.


Liz Burke Consulting uses an associate model - when working with Liz you will have access to other consultants and practioners who are experts in their specialised fields

Collection Development

Collection development is a foundation stone of any quality library service, whether it be e-resources, hard-copy, interesting ephemera, or living libraries. Achieving balance and relevance is key to a great collection.

Liz Burke Consultancy has the tools and expertise to help you build the best possible collection. We can assist you to build a collection that reflects the diversity of your current community, the historical nature of your place in the world, and the future needs for information, education, research and recreation.

Using statistical analysis, client surveys, user engagement, and rigorous collection evaluation we can help you to create a collection that is not only fit for purpose but has a plan to continuously improve. From e-resources, consortium deals, and hard-copy we can advise and negotiate the best outcomes for your library.

  • Client: Libraries
  • Date: March 2023
  • Service: Collection Development

Children, YA & Schools

Children are the future of any library service and they have a special place in public and school libraries.

Liz Burke Consulting has over 20 years experience working with children and young adults in Australia, New Zealand, and at international schools around the globe to improve literacy and language outcomes. We work with school librarians and teachers to improve information literacy and enthuse children and young adults from all cultures with a love of literature, reading, and storytelling. Our unique literacy programmes have been shared across 50 different countries covering Australasia, South America, Asia, and Europe.

We can help with all aspects of school and children's and YA library services including:
• helping you develop a new library for your school, or renew and update an existing library;
• advising on the best library system to suit your needs (and your budget);
• working with you to make your library an essential part of your school environment;
• showing your team how to catalogue non-fiction books using the Dewey system;
• helping you select titles for your collection;
• assisting with ideas for layout, displays and activities to keep your library service fresh and engaging.

Liz Burke Consulting provides the very best specialist advice when it comes to services for children and YA.

  • Client: Schools
  • Date: March 2023
  • Service: Schools
